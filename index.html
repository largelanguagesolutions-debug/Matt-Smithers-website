About

I work at the point where the plan meets the real world.

My career began at the wrench-turning and operator level, where consequences were immediate and systems knowledge was earned in the aircraft. I spent 22 years in naval aviation as a mechanic, Flight Engineer, instructor, and operational leader, logging more than 6,000 flight hours on P-3 Orion and C-130 aircraft.


I later moved into flight test, maintenance leadership, technical data, fleet support, and program readiness on major aerospace programs. The altitude changed, but the operating problem stayed remarkably consistent: capable but siloed functions often execute well individually while the enterprise struggles to integrate them around one executable readiness plan. This is the white space I operate in.


That is the work I do now. I expose dependencies early, clarify ownership, create operating models, establish readiness criteria, and build the procedures and feedback loops that make the next deployment more repeatable than the first. I am also applying that same systems mindset to software and product development focused on field operations intelligence.
